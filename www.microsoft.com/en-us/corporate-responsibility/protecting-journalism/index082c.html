    Preserving and protecting journalism

    The Microsoft journalism initiative works to help strengthen local news ecosystems, restore trust and confidence in news, and provides cyber and legal security for journalists.

    Read the blog

    Why invest in strengthening journalism?

    Microsoft launched our journalism initiative to help address the accelerating crisis facing journalism by looking at ways to provide journalists and newsrooms new tools, technology, and capacity to expand reach and efficiency for local news outlets.

    Rebuilding reporting capacity is critical as over 35,000 newspaper reporter jobs have been lost since 2010.

    We are helping newsrooms explore new hybrid business models, as their ad revenue has shrunk by $34 billion.

    We must stem the rise of news deserts, as the number of US counties without a local newspaper is 200 and climbing.

    Strengthening journalism in rural areas is crucial—70 percent of US counties without daily or weekly newspapers are rural.

    Our approach

    We believe that healthy democracies require healthy journalism. Technology has played a role in the disruption of news, but it can also be an important part of the rebuilding effort.

    Business

    The current business model for news organizations is under threat as advertising spend continues to flow to tech platforms instead of publishers, and as private equity firms buy up struggling publishers and then sell off or dismantle news operations.

    Trust

    News organizations aren’t just battling with a changing business model but with increasing distrust. As news organizations look forward, they are even more concerned about the impact of new tech-driven issues like disinformation, deep fakes, and responsible use of synthetic media.

    Security

    Around the world violence against journalists is on the rise, especially for those reporters covering conflict, human rights abuses, and corruption. Digital technology like Cloud Computing and AI may be able to play an important role in monitoring the safety of journalists, spotting threats, and helping ensure justice in the court of law.

    Where and how we work

    When choosing where to start, we looked at areas that faced challenges in their local news ecosystems, the diversity of each community, the strong support of local news, and referrals by third parties working on the future of news. Partnering from the beginning with the local community foundation in each area was critical to building the program and attracting the right set of initial partners.

    ‘Rebuilding Local News’, a pilot program

    We are working together with area colleges and universities, leaders and organizations, and their wider communities to provide critical support to local newsrooms and help them tell stories in new ways. At the same time, we’re experimenting with new revenue streams and funding models.

    Related programs

    Defending Democracy

    Working with stakeholders including governments, non-government organizations, academics, and industry all in democratic countries to protect campaigns from hacking; increase political advertising transparency online; protect electoral processes; and defend against disinformation campaigns.

    Learn about the program

    Protecting Journalists Pro Bono Program

    Partnering with DWT, RCFP and the Knight Foundation, we’re piloting the Protecting Journalists Pro Bono Program. It provides free legal support to journalists and small news organizations across three workstreams: pre-publication review, access to public records, and defending journalists against subpoenas. 

    Learn about our pro bono program

    Microsoft AccountGuard for Journalists

    A security service offered at no additional cost to news organizations, providing notification of nation-state cyberattacks. Helping newsrooms and journalists protect themselves from cybersecurity threats on M365 emails and personal accounts. Includes access to cybersecurity training and new security features.

     

    Learn more about AccountGuard
